<![CDATA[Expatin' In Amsterdam]]>http://amsterdam.alexakaminsky.com/Ghost 0.5Thu, 23 Oct 2014 21:59:48 GMT60<![CDATA[Purpose Of This Blog]]>For the past two years I have been using the app DayOne to keep track of my day to day activities. I have over 400 entires (not patting myself on the back or anything). I highly recommend it. I will continue journaling but I wanted to start this blog to go deeper than:

"I woke up at 9:30, went to work and had pizza for dinner."

What I have learned from journaling is that I love to look back on it weeks or months later. I love reading an old entry, having that memory brought back, and reliving it. Instead of just reliving those moments, I want to remember my thought process, explore my emotions, and gain insight from my experiences.

I am starting this blog because my life is about to drastically change: Starting a new job as a UX Designer with Booking.com; Moving to a new city / country / continent; Speaking a new language; Meeting new people; Figuring out a new government and rule system; Being open minded to trying new foods; And getting a new telephone number after 11 years.

I am not sure how long I will stay in Amsterdam. I say that I am giving it a maximum of 3 years but one never knows. 3 years ago I thought I was going to find a job in Atlanta and stay there after college. What I do know is what I hope to gain from this experience:

  • to become fully independent
  • to travel travel travel
  • to gain a worldly perspective
  • to learn conversational dutch
  • to develop connections with people from all over the world
  • to become better at my job
  • and to travel

Some things you should know if you are going to read this blog. I write like I talk. So if you are hoping to find some magnificently written blog this is not the place. If bad grammar bothers you, turn away now. I promise you I will mix up their and there, your and you're, and many many more. Also I am coding this site so there is no spell check. You've been warned.

Well that's it for now. The moving company is coming by my apartment tomorrow to do a pre-moving survey. Sh*ts gettings real.
